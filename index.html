Projects

3D Convolutional Model Training on Lung CT Scan Images

This project aimed at training a 3D convolutional neural network (CNN) on lung CT scan images to detect abnormalities such as tumors. The process involved multiple stages, from preprocessing the 3D CT scan images to training the CNN model and evaluating its performance.

To begin, I collected and preprocessed a dataset of CT scan images, ensuring that the data was correctly formatted for 3D convolution. I employed data augmentation techniques to enhance the dataset, which was crucial due to the limited availability of medical data.

The most challenging part was managing the complexity of 3D data. Unlike 2D images, 3D CT scans required significant computational power and memory. I overcame these challenges by using batch processing and optimizing the architecture of the CNN for better efficiency. I also had to carefully tune hyperparameters to achieve acceptable performance levels, balancing precision and recall to minimize false negatives in tumor detection.

Throughout the process, I gained a deeper understanding of the nuances of medical image processing and the unique challenges involved in training models on volumetric data. This experience has strengthened my skills in working with complex data and optimizing deep learning models for practical applications in medical fields.

Feature Analysis with Multimodal Model (IdeficsForVisionText2Text) using NASA HiRISE Data

In this project, I applied image processing techniques like normalization, resizing, and contrast adjustment to NASA's HiRISE dataset, which is used to analyze Martian surface structures. I fine-tuned the Idefics multimodal model (9B parameters) to detect and provide detailed information related to Martian images.

The challenge here was to effectively combine both text and image data to train the model. I had to experiment with different techniques to ensure that the model could process both modalities in a meaningful way. This project required extensive knowledge of both computer vision and natural language processing (NLP), and it helped me deepen my understanding of multimodal deep learning.

Alzheimer’s Prediction using Graph Neural Network

For this project, I developed a 5-layer Graph Convolutional Network (GCN) model to predict Alzheimer’s stages using diffusion tensor imaging (DTI) data. I converted the DTI data into a graph format and trained the GCN model to predict the stages of Alzheimer’s based on brain region connectivity matrices.

The biggest challenge here was to efficiently work with the graph-based data and extract meaningful features from the connectivity matrices. Despite these challenges, I was able to achieve 47% accuracy, which was a strong foundation for further development in brain disease prediction using graph-based neural networks.
